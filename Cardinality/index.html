Consent to act as a research subject

Edward Vul, Ph.D. is conducting a research study about mean position perception. You have been asked to participate because you are an undergraduate here at UCSD.

PROCEDURES. If you agree to participate in this study, the following will happen to you:
1. You will see a display of objects.
2. Your task is to estimate the average position of these objects.

RISKS. No potential risks or discomforts are anticipated except for the possibility that some tasks may be slightly boring.

PAYMENT/REMUNERATION. In consideration of your time and inconvenience, you will receive half hour of course credit. The procedures and length of participation will last approximately 15-20 minutes.

RIGHTS. You may call the UCSD Human Research Protection Program at 858-657-5100 to ask about your rights as a research subject or to report research-related problems.

BENEFITS. There will be no direct benefit to you from these procedures. However, the investigator may learn more about basic questions pertaining to attention, memory, perception, cognition.

EXPLANATION. The researcher has explained this study to you and answered your questions. If you have questions or research related problems, you may reach Edward Vul at 858-534-4401.

VOLUNTARY NATURE OF PARTICIPATION. Participation in research is entirely voluntary. You may refuse to participate or withdraw at any time without penalty.

CONFIDENTIALITY. Research records will be kept confidential to the extent allowed by law. As with all research, there is also the possibility of loss of confidentiality. Information from participants will be identified by a study number. The database which relates the study number to a specific subject will be maintained in the study coordinators office.

COPY OF CONSENT. You have received a copy of this consent document to keep and a copy of the Experimental Bill of Rights.

Welcome to the Mean Position Estimation study!

In each trial, after clicking "START" button, you will be presented with objects scattered on the display for 500ms. Three examples are shown below.

Your goal is to estimate the global mean position (equivalently, the average value of data points, the center of mass or the balancing point) of these objects by clicking on the display. A black cross will show up at the estimated position after your click. You have unlimited attempts for your responses by clicking on a different estimated position as you wish. Finalize your decision by clicking the "SUBMIT" button. You will not be able to change your response after clicking the "SUBMIT" button.

After finalizing the response and clicking the "SUBMIT" button, the button turns to "NEXT" and click for the next trial (The trial starts immediately after the click).

You will start with 6 practice trials in which the true mean position (the red cross) will be provided after your response.

However, you will not be provided with feedback in the experimental trials(i.e., you will not be informed about the true mean positions).

There will be total of 660 trials (6 practice trials + 6 attention-check trials + 648 experiment trials), one-minute break and a short survey (about 50mins). Please provide your most intuitive and best estimation!
